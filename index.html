🩸 Bloodwork Game Plan (Nov 2025 labs)

Ferritin 11 ng/mL (low) — iron stores have been low on three straight tests, even though hemoglobin is normal. In a calorie deficit with 5 training days, low iron shows up as heavy legs, a racing heart rate on cardio, and slow recovery. The meal plan leans on beef, dark-meat poultry, and tuna for heme iron, always paired with vitamin C. Keep coffee and tea an hour away from iron-rich meals — they block absorption. Ask her doctor about an iron supplement; at ferritin 11 food alone rebuilds stores slowly.

Vitamin D 29 ng/mL (just under sufficient) — up from 10 in 2024, so what she's doing is working; a daily D3 (2,000 IU is a common dose — confirm with her doctor) plus outdoor Saturday walks should get her over 30.

B12 357 pg/mL (low-normal) — fine, but worth watching since it trends with energy. Eggs, beef, and tuna in the plan cover it.

Everything else looks great: blood sugar (HbA1c 5.1), cholesterol, triglycerides, and inflammation (hsCRP 0.6) are all excellent. Recheck ferritin and vitamin D around week 12 to confirm they're climbing. This isn't medical advice — run supplements past her doctor.
